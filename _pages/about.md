Chan-Jin "CJ" Chung is a professor of computer science at Lawrence Technological University, Southfield, Michigan, USA. He's projects and programs include:

* Development of Intelligent Ground Vehicles and IGVC (Intelligent Ground Vehicles Competition) Team Faculty Advisor since 2003. <a href="https://ltu.edu/arts-and-sciences/department-of-math-and-computer-science/intelligent-ground-vehicle-competition/" target="_blank">Link</a>
* Autonomous Robotics Competition <a href="https://www.robofest.net/" target="_blank">Robofest</a>, Founder and Advisory Board Chair
* CS Robotics Lab later renamed as the
  <a href="https://ltu.edu/projects/car-cs-ai-robotics-lab/">CAR (CS AI Robotics)</a> Lab, Founding Director
* <a href="https://www.robofest.net/CSPA/" target="_blank">Math Dance</a>, Integrated STEaM and AI program for K-12 students
* Self-Drive Algorithm Development: an established workforce development model for upskilling next-generation engineering and CS talent based on the <a href="https://ltu.edu/arts-and-sciences/department-of-math-and-computer-science/nsf-reu/)" target="_blank">NSF REU (2022-2025)</a>
program specializing in the development of autonomous navigation algorithms and robust, multi-sensor perception AI models. 
* <a href="https://robofest.github.io/v2x-cybersecurity-ai/" target="_blank">AI and Cybersecurity for V2X</a>: Curriculum and applied R&D pathways addressing critical security vulnerability, data validation, and real-time network predictive AI within Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X) communication frameworks. 
* <a href="https://robofest.github.io/manufacturing-defect-ai/" target="_blank">Manufacturing Defect Detection using AI</a>: A practical deployment partnerships with a automotive industrial supplier, leveraging computer vision, edge computing, and real-time deep learning models to transition factories from traditional quality inspection to automated, predictive defect prevention.
